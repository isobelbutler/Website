Welcome to my application website, I'm Isobel.

Who I am. I’m a 26 year old coder-in-the-making living in South East London.

My name’s Isobel (she/her). For the past 4 years I’ve worked in the Art Department in TV. I left the industry this year after a final series on MasterChef as Assistant Art Director, and have since been building my application for Founders and Coders whilst working in a plant shop.

Weirdly enough, the TV industry was what rebooted my interested in coding. I interviewed with a company who were looking for a Runner. I’d always been tech savvy and as a teen I loved picking apart existing blog themes and making them my own. When I didn’t get the job because they wanted someone with more explicit tech skills, it motivated me to revisit those skills and create something I could showcase. I taught myself HTML and CSS and built myself a website from scratch.

I believe that language holds a lot of power. It’s at the core of human connection and through the smallest change in syntax you can completely alter meaning - I think this is beautiful. I studied English Language and Literature at the University of Leeds and believe that meaning is conveyed best through clear, concise language.

I’ve always loved visual design - whether playing with fonts and colours or redecorating a room. When I worked in TV I loved transforming a filming space as a team and then stepping back after to admire all our hark work. I also love setting myself DIY projects. Most recently I built hanging herb planters out of old wooden pallets found on East Street Market which I’m going to grow shady herbs in.

I find balance and joy through exercise - I cycle, jump rope, and am a regular gym-goer, it allows me to be kindly competitive with myself. This year I want to cycle the Coast to Coast and experiment with track cycling.

My core belief is that other people are what make life wonderful, that you grow through challenging yourself whilst listening to your boundaries, and that perseverance will get you a long way.

Why web development. It’s the perfect combination of challenging, satisfying, and engrossing.


I’ve always loved maths, logic, design, and language and for me, coding is at the intersect of all these pleasures.

I love the challenge of trying to figure out clean ways to make things more efficient. I’ve always been interested in computers and tech, I used to spend hours altering blog themes and have always been the go-to person to ask techie problems to at work and with my mates. When I left the TV Art Department I was keen to start working on building some websites. When I did this I realised that whilst I love visual design, the thing that brings me the most joy is the behind the scenes - of finding elegant ways to make everything work and coming up with neat solutions to the problems that occur along the way. There have been moments whilst building the application projects where I’ve literally cheered when I’ve finally gotten something to work.

Project Gallery.

Why Founders and Coders. It feels sacred to have a path into a classically exclusive industry with people who actively prioritise diversity.


The tech industry is famously homogenous in terms of it’s workforce and whilst companies are beginning to prioritise hiring more women, a lot of the time it can feel like performative inclusivity.

Entering the industry felt impenetrable both socially and monetarily until I heard about Founders and Coders from a previous cohort member. I’m a queer, neurodivergent woman and to have a learning environment which feels like it recognises and accepts people like me feels extremely special. It would be an honour to be a part of Founders and Coders.
