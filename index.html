Hello!

I'm Isobel.

Who I am. I’m a 26 year old coder-in-the-making living in South East London.


My name’s Isobel (she/her).

For the past four years I’ve worked in the Art Department in TV. I left the industry this year after a final series on MasterChef as Assistant Art Director, and have since been building my application for Founders and Coders whilst working in a plant shop.

Weirdly enough, the TV industry was what rebooted my interested in coding.

I interviewed with a company who were looking for a Runner. I’d always been tech savvy and as a teen I loved picking apart existing blog themes and making them my own. When I didn’t get the job because they wanted someone with more explicit tech skills, it motivated me to revisit those skills and create something I could showcase. I taught myself HTML and CSS and built myself a website from scratch.

Why web development. It’s the perfect combination of challenging, satisfying, and engrossing.


I’ve always loved maths, logic, design, and language and for me, coding is at the intersect of all these pleasures.

Tell us about why you are applying for the programme? Talk about why you’d like to move into a career as a web developer?

Why Founders and Coders. It feels sacred to have a path into a classically exclusive industry with people who actively prioritise diversity.


The tech industry is famously homogenous in terms of it’s workforce and whilst companies are beginning to prioritise hiring more women, a lot of the time it can feel like performative inclusivity.

Entering the industry felt impenetrable monetarily until I heard about Founders and Coders from a previous cohort member. I’m a queer woman and to have a learning environment which feels like it recognises and accepts people like me feels extremely special. It would be an honour to be a part of Founders and Coders.
